Research areas

Our research area primarily focuses on the engineering of catalysts for various processes, as well as the study of kinetics and efficiency across different aspects of these processes. Below are some of the major working areas we are currently focusing on.

Tri-reforming of methane

Steam and methane undergo catalytic reaction, yielding hydrogen and carbon monoxide, a key process in industrial hydrogen production.

Biomass valorization

Conversion of biomass into valuable products through chemical or biological processes for sustainable resource utilization.

CO2 utilization

The conversion of carbon dioxide (CO2) into valuable products for mitigating climate change and promoting sustainability.

NH3 utilization

The utilization of methane (CH4) for various applications, including energy production, chemical synthesis, and environmental remediation.

To collaborate with us...

To be considered for collaboration, it's essential to demonstrate proficiency in relevant chemical engineering principles and methodologies. This includes showcasing a proven track record of research or practical experience that aligns with our laboratory's focus areas. Additionally, evidence of innovative thinking and problem-solving skills applicable to chemical engineering challenges is highly valued. Equally important is a commitment to collaborative work, with the ability to effectively contribute to multidisciplinary teams. Strong communication skills are essential, enabling clear articulation of research ideas and findings. Together, these criteria ensure that collaborators bring valuable expertise and capabilities to our laboratory's projects, fostering a culture of innovation and collaboration.
